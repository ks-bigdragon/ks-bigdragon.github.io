How Points Work

Relic Points are earned in four distinct ways. Understanding each source changes how you prioritize targets during the battle.

First Control

One-time large bonus when your alliance captures a building for the first time. Very valuable — race for it early.

Occupation

Passive points over time while you hold a building. Swordshrine and Sanctums generate the most.

Loot Crates

Spawn when a building is captured from the enemy. Both sides can collect them — don't leave them on the ground.

Kill Points

Personal only. Offense: 80 pts per 10k soldier power defeated. Defense: 40 pts per 10k.

Scattered Points

When a building that has been held for a while is captured by the enemy, its accumulated occupation points scatter as Arsenal Supplies on the ground. Either side can collect them. Always send someone to pick them up after a building changes hands — this is a free point swing most players ignore.

Buildings & Priority

The map has 11 structures split across three phases. Not all are worth equal effort — know what to hit and when.

Phase 1 — Available from the start

1

Royal Stables

Reduces teleport cooldown by 50% (10 min → 5 min). Capture this first. Faster teleports means you can contest more buildings and react faster across the entire map.

2

Sanctums (×2)

Steady passive Relic Point generation. Located near the Swordshrine. Holding both gives a significant long-term point advantage.

3

Bell Tower

Reduces building capture time by 50%. Once held, your team captures every building faster — a significant snowball advantage.

4

Abbeys (×4)

Passive Relic Points over time, less than Sanctums. Fill these with defenders once your core buildings are secured.

⚔ Phase 2 — Unlocks at the 15-minute mark

★

Swordshrine (Center)

The highest point-generating structure on the map. Don't rush it early — it's constantly vulnerable to Mercenary attacks. Recommended: target it with coordinated rallies in the final 10 minutes only.

!

Hall of Reformation

Grants all your troops a 15% damage boost and 15% damage reduction. Extremely impactful in combat — this is arguably the highest-priority Phase 2 target for your combat team.

M

Mercenary Camp

Lets you send Mercenaries to attack enemy-occupied buildings without using your own marches. Coordinate with your attack team to use it when the enemy is garrisoning the Swordshrine.

Phase 3 — Random spawn, closing stage

Undercellars appear randomly on the map. They give large one-time bursts of both Personal and Alliance Relic Points. Find their locations using the magnifying glass icon on the minimap. Rush these as soon as they appear — they disappear quickly.

Team Roles

Split your 30 fighters into three teams before the battle starts. ~1/3 each. Plan this on Discord or voice chat.

A

Attackers (~8 players)

Your strongest fighters
  • Hit and weaken enemy players to reduce their troop counts
  • Focus down the enemy's strongest player first
  • When troops run low, retreat and heal (12 min cooldown) while Support covers
  • Coordinate rallies on the Swordshrine in the final 10 minutes
S

Support (~8 players)

Your whale + solid players
  • Help Defenders capture key buildings at the start
  • Cover attack duties when Attackers are retreating to heal
  • Pick up Loot Crates and farm Undercellars in Phase 3
  • Reinforce garrisons when defenders are outnumbered
D

Defenders (~14 players)

Everyone else including F2P
  • Garrison and hold captured buildings
  • Split into two groups: one holds Bell Tower + 1 Sanctum + 2 Abbeys, other holds Royal Stables + 1 Sanctum + 2 Abbeys
  • Reinforce each other when under attack
  • Use Troop Encampment Dance if your city is about to be burned (send troops to encamp nearby)

Phase-by-Phase Strategy

Phase 1 — Opening (0–15 min)

  • Support + Defenders rush Royal Stables and Bell Tower immediately
  • Grab at least one Sanctum and start securing Abbeys
  • Attackers hit enemy players to drain their troops — weaker defenders = easier building captures
  • R4/R5: place map markers now to guide players on targets
  • Remember: buildings need 3 minutes before full control is granted — don't leave before it completes

Phase 2 — Mid-game (15–50 min)

  • Immediately contest the Hall of Reformation — the 15% combat buff changes every fight
  • Defenders split across buildings: half on Bell Tower + 1 Sanctum + 2 Abbeys, other half on Royal Stables + 1 Sanctum + 2 Abbeys
  • Use the Mercenary Camp to probe enemy-held buildings without burning your own marches
  • Attackers who healed rejoin the fight — Support returns to building support and crate collection
  • Hold off on the Swordshrine — too exposed to Mercenary attacks, not worth the early commitment

🔴 Phase 3 — Endgame (Final 10 min)

  1. 1 Watch for Undercellars appearing on the minimap — send fast movers immediately
  2. 2 Attackers teleport in circles around the Swordshrine before committing
  3. 3 Attack team starts a rally on Swordshrine — Support team follows with a second rally a few seconds later
  4. 4 Keep staggered rallies going until captured — then garrison heavily, the enemy will counter immediately
  5. 5 Collect all scattered Arsenal Supplies — don't let the enemy pick them up after buildings change hands

Critical Rules

Only Register Players Who Will Actually Show Up

If 30 players are registered but only 15 log in, matchmaking pits your reduced roster against a full enemy Legion. This is almost always unwinnable. Leadership must confirm attendance before clicking register — not after.

Healing is Free — Don't Waste Speedups

All Swordland injuries are healed for free when you leave the battlefield. Batch healing does not work here — only healing speedups apply. The correct move when wiped is to tap the VS banner → Leave Battlefield, wait 12 minutes, and rejoin fully healed.

Save your healing speedups for other events. Do not waste them here.

Free March Speed & Combat Buffs

  • You receive free 25% march speed throughout the event (1 speedup per minute, capped at 10) — don't hoard them, use them freely
  • 10% or 20% combat buffs (bought with gems) multiply your power, not add — if you have 500% attack, a 20% buff gives you 600%, not 520%
  • Activate all pet skills, governor gear bonuses, and hero buffs before the battle starts

Attacking is Often Better Than Defending

When an enemy is garrisoning a building, burning their city forces them to teleport away to defend — this is often faster and more effective than rallying the building directly. Track enemy marches: if one player is reinforcing + attacking + garrisoning, they have 3 marches out, meaning their city is nearly undefended.

Counter-recon tip: Start a rally on a suspected whale to see how many reinforcements they have, then cancel before it launches.

When You're in a Lopsided Matchup

If your alliance heavily outmatches the opponent (or vice versa), coordinate to trade buildings back and forth. This spawns Loot Crates and generates kill points from combat, maximizing Personal Relic Points for both sides when the outcome is already decided. If you're losing badly, use the guerrilla loop: leave → heal → rejoin → fight → repeat to maximize your personal rewards from the Loser's Bracket.

Rewards

Participation earns tokens for the Swordland Shop. Your bracket (Winner/Loser) determines token quantity — but even losing gives meaningful rewards worth showing up for.
