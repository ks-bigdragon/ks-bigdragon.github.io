Damage Mechanics

Understanding how the damage formula works separates average alliances from those consistently hitting the 1.2B cap.

Why Lethality > Attack

Both are multipliers in the damage formula. But most players' Lethality starts far lower than Attack — every point of Lethality added produces a larger proportional boost. If your Attack is 10,000 and Lethality is 2,000, adding 500 Lethality = +25% multiplier. Adding 500 Attack = only +5%. Same resource investment, dramatically different result.

Bear Battle Structure

The bear consists of 5,000 special infantry troops with 10 Defense and 83.33 Health each. Battles run for exactly 10 rounds. Your troops don't die, so damage per round stays constant (barring chance-based hero skills). Archers deal +10% extra from Ranged Strike vs. infantry bear troops.

Damage Formula (Full)

Full kills formula:

Kills = √Troops × (Attack × Lethality) / (Enemy Defense × Enemy Health) × SkillMod

SkillMod breakdown:

SkillMod = (DamageUp × OppDefenseDown) / (OppDamageDown × DefenseUp)

Per-troop attack vs bear:

base_attack × (1 + attack_bonus) × lethality / 100

Total battle damage:

sum of all 10 rounds × 3 troop types (Archer gets ×1.10)

Expedition Skills vs Widget (Exclusive Skills)

Expedition Skills

Additive — stack linearly. Chenko's +25% Lethality is added, not multiplied.

Widget Skills ⚡

Can be multiplicative — scales proportionally with your existing stats. Higher stats = bigger Widget gain.

Widgets only activate from the Rally Leader slot. That's why Helga in slot 2–3 wastes her biggest strength — her Widget never fires.

Diminishing returns on Archers: The formula uses √troop_count, meaning 100% Archers gives diminishing returns. Infantry and Cavalry activate hero troop-type buff skills — removing them loses those multipliers entirely. Minimum percentages exist to trigger those buffs, not for their own damage contribution.

📊 Your Personal Damage Score Depends on Whose Rally You Join

As a joiner, your damage points come from your troops — but the stats applied to those troops come entirely from the rally leader's stats. Joining the strongest rally leader = higher personal damage with the same troops. Actively seek out rallies from the most powerful players in the alliance, not just the nearest location.

The Joiner Rule

The game picks the top 4 highest-level joiner skills per rally and ignores the rest. If your first hero has a weak or defensive skill, you are physically replacing a slot that Chenko could occupy. Sending zero heroes is far better — your troops still contribute fully, and you don't block a better skill.

⚡ Mythic Heroes: 3 Expedition Skills vs Epic: 2 Skills

Mythic (Gold) heroes have 3 expedition skills — Epic heroes only have 2. That one extra skill makes a significant difference for rally leaders. Mythic heroes also have Widgets, which are multiplicative and scale with your existing stats — the higher your stats, the bigger the Widget gain. This is why Helga and Amadeus as rally leaders are so far ahead of Epic alternatives, even at the same star level.

Skill Level 5 always wins — even a bad skill. The game prioritizes joiner skills by level first, then join order. Howard at Level 5 (DefenseUp) will displace Chenko at Level 4. Always max your joiner skill to Level 5 before using it as a joiner. An S-tier hero that hasn't been maxed can be bumped by any maxed garbage hero.

P2W Rally Leader — Deep Dive

Best lineup per generation · Alternatives · Widget logic

Rally leaders gain the most from Mythic (Gold) heroes because Widgets only activate from the leader slot and are multiplicative.

⚡ Widget Only Activates from Leader

Mythic heroes in slot 2–3 — yours or a joiner's — do not activate their widgets. Helga in slot 2 while Amadeus leads = Helga's Lethality Widget never fires. She only contributes her expedition skills — the same value as any Epic hero.

🚫 Why Not Amadeus · Helga · Saul?

Helga's Widget is inactive in slot 2–3. You gain nothing compared to having Jabel in that slot — and you lose the option for Helga to lead a separate rally with her Widget fully active.

Rally Leader Responsibilities

Being a rally leader isn't just about picking the right heroes. Leaders have active responsibilities that directly impact every member's damage.

1

Activate ALL buffs before launching

Pets, consumable combat buffs, cosmetic bonuses (skins, frames, island decorations) — all apply to the entire rally. Careless buff activation hurts every joiner.

2

Monitor yellow flag — kick bad joiners & educate

If a member joins with Jabel/Howard/Gordon in slot 1, kick them and explain in chat. A Level 5 defensive skill displaces Chenko at Level 4. 10 seconds of education beats a silent kick — it builds a better alliance culture long-term.

3

Teleport near the Pitfall before the event

March time = wasted event time. A rally with a 2-minute march that hits the bear 10× beats a 6-minute march rally that only hits 5×. Leaders must cluster near the Pitfall so joiners can find them easily.

4

Calculate & announce troop cap per joiner

1. Strongest host Rally Capacity (e.g. 1,132,210)

2. Subtract host Squad Capacity (e.g. 162,210)

3. Available joiner space = 970,000

4. Divide by 14 joiner slots = ~69,285

5. Cap per joiner = 69,000 troops

Post this number in chat before the event. Strong players get capped, the rally stays open longer, and all members can participate.

5

Save formation preset before event day

Bear Hunt is not the time to adjust troops manually. A saved preset = join in 10 seconds without errors. Update whenever you change generation or after a major troop upgrade.

★

Star / Bookmark Rally Feature

Use the star icon inside any rally screen to bookmark it. The bookmarked rally always floats to the top of your rally list — no scrolling, no confusion under time pressure. Lets joiners instantly re-join the strongest whale rally the moment their troops return.

Arrow Math

Every member receives 24 arrows/day from Intel Missions. 40 members × 24 × 2 days = 1,920 arrows — enough to max the Pitfall every cycle.

Stop at Level 5

Extra arrows after Level 5 are not saved — they're wasted. R4/R5 should announce in chat once the cap is reached.

Intel Mission completion must be a clear alliance expectation, not a casual suggestion. One member skipping daily arrows looks small. But with 40 members being inconsistent over 2 days, that gap is the difference between Level 3 and Level 5 Pitfall — a 10–15% damage penalty for the entire alliance before the first rally even launches.

Critical Rules

1. Check the Yellow Flag Before Joining

Every rally has a yellow flag menu showing the 4 active joiner skills. Check it before confirming. If all 4 DamageUp slots are already filled, just send troops — or use a blue hero for capacity. If you see a defensive hero in the top 4, contact the rally leader to kick. Takes 5 seconds.

2. Rally Leaders Must Be Close to the Pitfall

March time = wasted event time. Only open a rally if your city is within 5 minutes of the Pitfall. Strong rally leaders must teleport near the trap before the event starts. A rally with a 2-minute march hitting the bear 10× beats one near the Pitfall that only hits 5×.

3. R4/R5: Manage the Number of Concurrent Rallies

Too many simultaneous rallies spreads joiners too thin. The math strongly favors a few well-filled rallies over many diluted ones. If your alliance has 40 players and 20 rallies open at once, none of them will get the optimal 2 Chenko + 2 Amane combo.

Joiner cap trick: R4/R5 can limit troops per join (e.g. max 20,000). This forces more joiners per rally, distributes hero skills correctly, and prevents one whale from filling an entire rally slot alone.

4. Formation Preset — Save Before Event Day

Set up your Bear Hunt formation (the correct Infantry/Cavalry/Archer ratio for your gen) as a saved preset. Bear Hunt is not the time for manual troop adjustments. A ready preset = join in 10 seconds with no errors. Update every time you change generation or after a major troop upgrade.

5. Schedule Around Active Players, Not Whales

The strongest TC30 whale in the perfect Pitfall position means nothing if they're asleep. Run two Hunting Traps and poll members on available time slots. Choose the two slots with the most confirmed attendance — a slightly weaker setup that fills rallies quickly beats a perfect setup with half the alliance offline.
