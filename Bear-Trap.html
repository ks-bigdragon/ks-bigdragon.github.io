The bear does not fight back. No injuries, no troop losses, nothing. Every single defensive stat on your account is completely worthless in this event. The only two stats that matter are Lethality and Attack — and Lethality matters more.

Damage Mechanics

Understanding how the damage formula actually works is what separates average alliances from those consistently hitting the 1.2B cap.

Why Lethality > Attack

Both stats are multipliers in the damage formula. But most players' Lethality starts far lower than their Attack, meaning every point of Lethality added produces a larger proportional damage boost than the same point of Attack. Prioritize Lethality in research, gear, and charms above everything else.

Bear Battle Structure

The bear consists of 5,000 special infantry troops. Battles run for exactly 10 rounds — not until one side is wiped. Your troops don't die, so your damage per round stays constant (barring chance-based hero skills). Archers deal +10% extra damage from Ranged Strike vs. infantry bear troops.

Damage Formula (simplified)

Per-troop attack:

base_attack × (1 + attack_bonus) × lethality / 100

Round damage per troop type:

√(troop_count × bear_count) × attack_per_troop / bear_defense / 100 × skillmod

Total battle damage:

sum of all 10 rounds × 3 troop types (Archer gets ×1.10)

Diminishing returns on Archers: The formula uses the square root of troop count, which means stacking 100% Archers gives diminishing returns. That's why keeping minimum Infantry and Cavalry matters — they activate their hero's troop-specific buff skills, which multiplies their small count into meaningful damage. Going 100% Archers actually loses you those big hero multipliers.

Troop Formation — By Generation

The optimal troop split shifts heavily toward Archers as you progress. This is because Archer heroes gain stronger stats each generation and Archers deal the most raw damage. Most guides give you 10/10/80 and call it done — the actual endgame standard is different.

Gen 2 10% 20% 70% Archer heroes catching up
Gen 3 10% 10% 80% Most players stop here — don't

Hard floor — keep at least 5,000 Infantry in absolute numbers, even at Gen 4+. The damage formula punishes dropping below this disproportionately. Calculate the absolute count, not just the percentage ratio — a 1% split on a small army can fall below this floor without you realizing.
Finding your personal optimum at Gen 8+: When your Archer Lethality exceeds your Cavalry Lethality by more than ~358 points, nudge Cavalry down in 5% steps and Archers up. Watch estimated single-hit damage on the march screen — the split that gives the highest number is your current optimum. Redo this after any major gear or research upgrade.

The Joiner Rule

As a joiner, the game picks the top 4 highest-level joiner skills per rally and ignores the rest entirely. If your first hero has a weak or defensive skill, you are physically displacing a slot that could've held someone else's Chenko. Sending no hero at all is genuinely better — your troops still contribute fully, and you don't block a better skill from being counted.

Pitfall Upgrade System

The Pitfall must reach Level 5 before the first rally launches. The +25% Attack bonus applies to every troop in every rally for the full 30 minutes. Not maxing it is a full alliance damage penalty — it's not optional.

Arrow Math

Each member earns 24 arrows/day from Intel Missions. 40 active members × 24 × 2 days = 1,920 arrows — enough to max the Pitfall every cycle.

Stop at Level 5

Extra arrows after Level 5 are not banked for the next event — they're wasted. R4/R5 must post in chat when the cap is hit so members don't burn arrows unnecessarily.

Intel Mission completion must be a clear alliance expectation, not a casual suggestion. One member skipping their daily arrows is small. Across 40 members with inconsistent completion over 2 days, the gap is the difference between a Level 3 and Level 5 Pitfall — a real 10–15% damage penalty across every rally before anyone even launches.

Pets — What Helps, What Doesn't

Activate these before the event starts. Pet skills are a free damage boost most players forget to use — and some popular pets do nothing here.

✓

Mighty Bison — Squad Capacity +15,000 (2h)

More troops per march = more damage per rally. Critical for rally hosts who fill large rallies.

✓

Black Panther — Troop Lethality +10% (2h)

Direct damage buff. Lethality is the stat you want most — this is the best combat pet for this event.

✓

Giant Rhino — All Squad Attack +10% (2h)

Solid flat attack multiplier across all troop types. Use if Black Panther is on cooldown.

✗

Moose — only the Rally Capacity variant works

The Moose's Health-based skill is completely useless here because the bear has no health bar to attack — it only has Defense. Many players activate Moose expecting damage — it does nothing unless you're using the Rally Capacity variant specifically.

Critical Rules

1. Check the Yellow Flag Before Joining

Inside every rally there's a yellow flag menu that shows which 4 joiner skills are currently active. Check it before confirming your join. If 4 Chenkos are already present and you're bringing a 5th, your skill won't be counted — send troops only or use a blue hero for capacity. Takes 5 seconds and prevents the single most common joiner mistake.

2. Rally Leaders Must Be Near the Pitfall

March time is wasted event time. Only launch rallies if your city is within 5 minutes of the Pitfall. Strong rally leaders should teleport close to the trap before the event starts — forming a visible cluster makes it easier for joiners to find and fill rallies quickly. A rally with 2-minute march time that hits the bear 10 times beats a nearby rally that only hits 5 times.

3. R4/R5: Manage How Many Rallies Run Simultaneously

Running too many rallies at once spreads joiners thin. Each rally needs to fill with S-tier joiner heroes to hit peak damage. If your alliance has 40 players and 20 rallies open at once, no rally will get 4 Chenkos. The math strongly favors fewer, fully-stacked rallies over many diluted ones.

Joiner restriction trick: R4/R5 can tell joiners to cap their troop send (e.g. max 20,000 troops per join). This forces more joiners per rally, distributes damage more evenly, and prevents one whale from filling an entire rally slot alone. This solves the "instant fill" problem and is used by top alliances.

4. Use Formation Presets — Save Before the Event

Set up your Bear Hunt formation (correct Infantry/Cavalry/Archer split for your gen) as a saved Formation Preset. Bear Hunt is not the time to manually adjust troops. Having the preset ready means you can join rallies in 10 seconds flat without errors. Update your preset every time you change generations or after major troop upgrades.

5. Schedule for Your Active Players, Not Your Whales

A top TC30 whale at a perfect Pitfall location matters nothing if they're asleep. Run two Hunting Traps (your alliance gets two) and poll members on their available time slots. Pick the two slots with the most confirmed attendance — even a slightly weaker formation that fills rallies quickly beats a perfect setup with half your alliance offline.

Rewards

Bear Trap is the single most important recurring event for gear progression. Every Forge Hammer you'll ever need for hero gear upgrades comes from here. Miss events consistently and your gear falls behind in ways that are hard to recover from.

Forge Hammers

Primary crafting material for hero gear upgrades — no other event matches this source

Resources

Food, wood, stone, iron — amount scales with alliance damage tier

Alliance Tokens

200 tokens per Hunting Arrow donated — usable in Alliance Shop

Personal Damage Rewards

Individual bonus tier based on your personal damage contribution, separate from alliance total

Low spenders typically deal around 1–1.5M personal damage in the first weeks. Higher spenders scale significantly above this. The per-rally cap scales with your total invested stats — focusing on Archer Lethality research and gear pays off directly here before any other event.
